Sometimes parts of words change, for example capital letters at the beginning of sentences. The changing part is put into braces. The fix part is outside of the braces. This way, you can cover several spellings with sets.

An example: [tT]he matches the and The. The capital and the small T are between the braces to find upper and lower case spelling. Now, find the word you at the beginning and middle of the sentence by using [yY].

You can find the braces next to the Enter key on the right side of the keyboard.
